# GoNeo  
GoNeoWS provides functions written in Go for querying near Earth objects from the NeoWS
provided by the NASA JPL Asteroid Team. 

## Definitions 
Below the definitions to astronomical terms used in this module. 
Credit to NASA CNEOS for the definitions.
https://cneos.jpl.nasa.gov/glossary/h.html

NEO: Near Earth Object, an asteroid or comet with a perihelion distance of < 1.3 au.

au: Astronomical Unit is the approximate distance between the Earth and Sun (150 million Km).

AbsoluteMagnitudeH: H (absolute magnitude) is the visual magnitude an observer would record if the 
asteroid were placed 1 au away from the Sun at a 0 angle phase. 

lunar: lunar distance (LD) refers to the average distance between the Earth and the Moon of about 384400 km.
